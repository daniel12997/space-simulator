# Park et al. (2021) — JPL DE440 and DE441 ephemerides

The current operational JPL planetary and lunar ephemerides — **the canonical choice for Apsis per REQ-ENV-001** ("ephemerides for Sun, Moon, and major planets via SPICE SPK kernels (DE440 or later)"). Two variants from the same fit:

- **DE440**: spans 1550-2650. Recommended for "modern data" — includes lunar viscous-damping between liquid core and solid mantle, fits LLR data accurately.
- **DE441**: spans -13200 to +17191 (~30 millennia). No lunar viscous damping (avoids backward-integration divergence). Less accurate than DE440 for the current century, more accurate over very long historical spans.

Apsis should use **DE440 by default**; DE441 only for explicit paleo / future-millennial studies (out of normal mission profile).

## What's new vs DE421/DE430

- **Frame**: referred to **ICRF3** ([[sources/charlot-2020-icrf3]]) rather than the original ICRF. Inner-planet orbit orientation aligned to ICRF3 with ~0.2 mas average accuracy via Mars-spacecraft VLBI.
- **Jupiter orbit improved substantially** by Juno radio range and VLBA data (2016-2020).
- **Saturn orbit improved** by Cassini radio range and VLBA data (2004-2018).
- **Pluto orbit improved** by stellar occultations reduced against Gaia.
- **Lense-Thirring effect from the Sun** added to the dynamical model (§1).
- **Vondrak precession** for Earth orientation (Vondrak et al. 2011), more accurate than Lieske 1979 beyond ±1000 yr.
- **30 KBOs + circular Kuiper-belt ring** (36 point masses at 44 au) added — accounts for the long-period perturbation of inner planets by trans-Neptunian mass distribution.
- **343 asteroids** (≈90% of belt mass) included via n-body integration.

## Time scale and frame details

- Integrated using TDB (Barycentric Dynamical Time) per IAU 2006 Resolution B3.
- Eq. 3 gives the full TT ↔ TDB conversion (Soffel et al. 2003 + Petit-Luzum 2009 form), including periodic and Lense-Thirring terms — accurate to navigation requirements.
- Solar system barycentre defined per Estabrook 1971 (Eq. 1) with the PPN mass parameter `μ_i*` (Eq. 2) including kinetic-energy and gravitational-potential corrections to the rest-mass.

## Reported accuracies (DE440 vs DE441 differences)

- Lunar orbit (DE440 vs DE441) within 2 m over 1970-2020; grows along-track on longer time spans.
- Planet orbits (DE440 vs DE441) within 1 m over 1100-yr DE440 span.
- Planet orbit orientations to ICRF3 within ~0.2 mas average.

## Apsis relevance

- **REQ-ENV-001**: ephemerides for Sun, Moon, and major planets via SPICE SPK kernels (DE440 or later). DE440 is the canonical choice; download `de440.bsp` (or the dual-coverage `de440s.bsp`) from `naif.jpl.nasa.gov/pub/naif/generic_kernels/spk/planets/`.
- **Subsystems §1.3 and §2.2** (third-body gravity from Sun/Moon/planets via SPICE) — DE440 is what the SPICE queries return.
- The TDB↔TT formula (Eq. 3) is what SOFA's `iauDtdb` implements internally; Apsis delegates.
